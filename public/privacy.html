SkyMap Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 17, 2026
The short version: SkyMap has no accounts, no analytics, and no tracking. Your location is used only on your device, only while you use the app, and is never stored or sent anywhere.

Location

SkyMap asks for location permission ("while using the app") to show where you are on the skyway map and follow your progress along a route. Your coordinates are processed entirely in memory on your device. They are never saved, never transmitted to us (we have no server that could receive them), and never shared with anyone. Declining location access disables only those features — everything else works normally.

What the app stores on your device

  • Your five most recent destinations (building names), so search can offer them again.
  • A parking ramp you explicitly chose to save, so you can route back to it.
  • The app's own files and map data, cached for offline use.

All of this stays in your device's local storage. Deleting the app (or clearing website data, for the web version) removes it completely.

Network requests the app makes

  • Map tiles from OpenFreeMap — like any online map, the tile server sees your IP address and which map areas you view. OpenFreeMap states it does not log or track users.
  • Landmark photos hosted by Wikimedia Commons, fetched only when you open a landmark's card.
  • The app itself and its data from our static hosting. Skyway data comes from OpenStreetMap and contains no personal information.

That is the complete list. There are no analytics services, ad networks, or third-party trackers of any kind.

Feedback

The Feedback and Report buttons open your own email app with a pre-filled draft. Nothing is sent unless you choose to send it, and it goes through your email provider, not through us.

Changes

If SkyMap ever adds a feature that changes any of the above, this policy will be updated first and the "last updated" date bumped.
